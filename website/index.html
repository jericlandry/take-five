The science

Social connection
is healthcare.

The research is unambiguous. Keeping your parent connected — to family, to stories, to life — is one of the most powerful health interventions available. Take Five is built on this science.

29%

Increased risk of premature death from social isolation — comparable to smoking or obesity (National Academies, 2020)

50%

Increased risk of dementia among older adults experiencing chronic loneliness or isolation (HHS, 2023)

15

Cigarettes per day — the equivalent mortality impact of being socially disconnected (U.S. Surgeon General, 2023)

1 in 2

U.S. adults report measurable loneliness — declared a public health epidemic (Surgeon General's Advisory, 2023)

"Social connection is a significant predictor of longevity and better physical, cognitive, and mental health, while social isolation and loneliness are significant predictors of premature death and poor health."

The problem

Coordination collapse forces people
out of their homes.

People end up in managed care not because they have to — but because the logistics became unmanageable, and the connections that keep them healthy were allowed to fade.

01
One person carries everything

One exhausted adult child manages schedules, meds, and decisions — often from a distance, alongside siblings with varying involvement.

02
Tasks fall through the cracks

No shared visibility means no shared accountability. Things get missed. Crises happen that didn't have to.

03
Connection slowly fades

As logistics take over, the human relationship suffers. The visits get shorter. The isolation grows — quietly, dangerously, with real health consequences.

04
Stories are lost forever

Every person over 80 is a living archive. Without someone asking the questions, those stories disappear when they do.

How it works

Simple for everyone.
Powerful for families.

No new apps. No training. No friction. Take Five sits in your existing family group chat and holds everything together.

1
Set up your care ensemble

Invite family, professional caregivers, and anyone in your parent's support network to the group chat. Take Five joins quietly.

2
Everyone talks like they already do

After a visit, caregivers text naturally — "She ate well, loved her book, seemed happy today." No app. No form. Just a message in the group.

3
The agent reads and remembers

Take Five parses every update, maintains the care log, and stores what matters — medications, appointments, patterns — so it's never buried in scroll.

4
Family gets a weekly digest

Each week: what happened, how she's been, what needs a decision. The child in Denver stays genuinely in the loop.

5
Ask it anything

What medications is Dad on? When did the aide last come? The agent answers from everything shared — no digging through old messages.

6
The Story grows over time

Each week the agent asks one gentle question. The answers build into a memoir — her life, in her own words, preserved forever.

The agent, in action

The group chat.
Working harder.

The digest is one side of Take Five. The other is the agent — sitting right there in the family group chat, ready when someone has a question. Here's a real exchange, with names and details changed.

Alex9:48 AM

@TakeFive do any of Dad's medications have an effect on his sleep?

Take Five9:48 AM
Dad's Medications & Sleep Effects
Yes — several of his medications can meaningfully affect sleep. Here's a breakdown:
✅ Supports sleep

Mirtazapine 15 mg (evening) — strongly sedating, likely intentional. Prescribed partly for sleep and anxiety. The most sleep-promoting medication he takes.

⚠️ May disrupt sleep

Duloxetine 30 mg (morning) — SNRIs can cause insomnia or lighter sleep in some people, especially if timing shifts.

Metoprolol Succinate 25 mg (evening) — beta-blockers are known to suppress melatonin and can cause insomnia or unusual dreams. Evening dosing may amplify this.

Bottom line

The mirtazapine is the heavy hitter for sleep support. But the metoprolol (evening dosing) and duloxetine are both worth flagging with his doctors if he's reporting poor sleep quality or unusual dreams.

Is there a specific sleep concern you're looking into? That might help narrow this down further.

Take Five is reading all of this. The medication question gets answered in seconds. The doctor visit update — Dad's doing well, walked the treadmill, watching the soda — goes into the care log automatically. Sara's Friday visit lands on the calendar. None of it requires anyone to file anything.
